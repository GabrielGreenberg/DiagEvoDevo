# CONCEPT.md — The Theory

The scoring function is a formal account of when a figure is a good **homomorphism**
of a dataset: when geometric relations among the figure's elements track data relations
among the values. Everything below is the content the code must realize faithfully.

---

## 1. Goal

Generate diagrams by *optimization against a principled score*, not by imitation. Given a
dataset, a random configuration of line segments should evolve — under the score alone —
into a faithful diagram. We want diagram *kinds* to emerge as optima. The bar chart is
the first checkable target; the architecture must not privilege it beyond that.

## 2. The two structures being compared

A dataset and a figure are each a **relational structure**: a set plus relations on it.
Scoring measures how faithfully the figure's relations carry the data's.

### Data
- Twelve items, labelled **A, B, C, … L** (we use letters, not month names).
- Two independent relation families, one per dimension:
  - **Order** on the labels: A < B < … < L. Scale type: **ordinal**.
  - **Value** for each item, a positive real (dollar amount). Scale type: **ratio**
    (true zero; "twice as much" is meaningful).
- It is a **product structure** `⟨labels, <⟩ × ⟨values, ratio⟩`: the two families do not
  interact structurally even though values may covary with order.
- A **data seed** generates the twelve positive values. The label order is fixed.

### Figure
- Twelve **line segments**. Each segment = start point + end point = **4 numbers**.
  The whole figure is a **48-dimensional parameter vector**. This vector *is* the thing
  the optimizer moves.
- A **figure seed** generates a random initial 48-vector.

### The label correspondence does no quantitative work
Each segment carries a label; the label fixes a one-to-one pairing (bijection) between
the twelve data items and the twelve segments. It carries identity only, never magnitude.
After pairing we index both sides A…L and compare item-A to segment-A, etc. Labels are
never an argument to any fidelity term; they are the precondition that lets the terms be
written.

## 3. The measurement stock (per line)

From one segment we can read a fixed, enumerable set of scalar measurements, generated by
three choices:

- **Anchor** (2): the **page** (supplies a direction, *no origin*) or a **posited frame**
  (supplies *origin + direction*). The origin is the entire scale-type story.
- **Part** (4): **start**, **end**, **midpoint**, or the **displacement** (`end − start`,
  the segment taken as a self-anchored vector).
- **Reading** (4): **proj∥** (projection along the direction), **proj⊥** (across it),
  **magnitude** (length of the displacement-to-point, direction-free), **angle** (bearing).

Raw grid: **2 × 4 × 4 = 32** cells per line.

**Undefined cells (6):** on the page, a *point* (start/end/midpoint) has no origin to
measure a magnitude or bearing from → `page × {start,end,midpoint} × {magnitude, angle}`
= 6 undefined. (The displacement is self-anchored, so its magnitude = length and
angle = tilt remain defined even on the page.)

**Live measurements: 32 − 6 = 26 per line.** Scale-type census:

| scale type | count | examples |
|---|---|---|
| ratio | 15 | length; distances from a posited origin; run/rise (differences → true zero) |
| interval | 6 | x/y position of a point on the page (no origin → only differences) |
| cyclic | 5 | tilt; bearings from a posited origin |

Two coincidences to remember: **length is anchor-free** (displacement magnitude is
identical under both anchors), so the 26 live types resolve to **25** genuinely distinct
numbers; run/rise/tilt also coincide across anchors *unless* the posited frame is rotated
relative to the page.

### Scale type is the payload
Every cell is stamped with the structure it can carry, read off the frame:
- **origin only → ratio** magnitude (rotation-proof, the least a ratio reading needs);
- **origin + direction → ratio** projection and **cyclic** angle;
- **direction, no origin (the page) → interval** projection only.
A **difference** of two positions always has a true zero, which is why run, rise, and
length are ratio even on a bare page — and why a bar carries proportion with no drawn axis.

## 4. Lifting to twelve lines

- The figure's **48** parameters are the only independent numbers.
- Each of the 26 live measurements, applied across the twelve lines, is a **12-vector**
  (one value per line). There are 12 × 26 = **312** live measurement-values, all
  deterministic functions of the 48.
- **The 12-vector is the atomic unit of comparison.** All 66 = C(12,2) pairwise relations
  of a measurement (longer-than, ratio-of, same-as) are *functions of the twelve
  marginals*, so we never loop over pairs — a fidelity term compares two 12-vectors.

### The funnel (share this with a colleague)
`48 free parameters → 312 live measurements → 67 typed comparisons → 2 assigned → 4 numbers → 1 score`
where **67** = the mode-capped measurement×mode combinations (see §6), and the pair axis
collapses into the marginals.

## 5. Like-with-like (commensurability)

A scored comparison ranges over **one measurement held fixed across all twelve lines** —
lengths to lengths A…L, angles to angles A…L. Cross-type comparisons ("A's length vs B's
angle") are forbidden and, by construction, **unrepresentable**: the reading is a property
of the *comparison*, not of the *line*. One reading per comparison, swept over all lines.

This is not an extra rule; it follows from making the 12-vector atomic. Its justification
is **commensurability**: a comparison has a truth-value only if both sides share a space,
units, and scale type. This is the *same* principle as the data-side scale match in §6 —
applied once *within* the figure vector (all 12 entries one reading) and once *across* the
figure–data boundary (figure vector and data vector same scale type).

## 6. The fidelity ladder (the graded score)

For an assigned figure measurement `c ∈ ℝ¹²` and a data vector `v ∈ ℝ¹²`, fidelity is
graded on **nested rungs of increasing strictness**. The rungs nest downward: perfect
ratio ⇒ perfect interval ⇒ perfect order. Partial credit = "captured the ordinal
structure but not the ratio structure."

**Ordinal rung** — order agreement.
- Exact: `F_ord = (concordant pairs) / (total pairs)`, ties counted 0.5. Equivalently
  `(Kendall τ + 1)/2`. `= 1` iff `c` is any monotone-increasing function of `v`.
- Differentiable surrogate:
  `F_ord ≈ mean_{i<j} σ( sign(v_i − v_j) · (c_i − c_j) / T )`, `σ` logistic, `T` temperature.

**Interval rung** — spacing agreement.
- `F_int = r²(c, v)` (squared Pearson correlation) `= 1` iff `c = a·v + b` for some `a>0,b`.
  Correlation's affine-invariance *is* interval-scale invariance.

**Ratio rung** — proportion agreement (requires `c, v > 0`).
- Defect `= Var_i( log c_i − log v_i )`. `F_ratio = exp( −Var(log c − log v) / σ₀² ) ∈ (0,1]`,
  `= 1` iff `c = k·v` for some `k>0`. Invariant to the figure's overall scale `k`, as
  fidelity should be; `log` repels degenerate (zero-length) segments.

**Nesting check (must hold, is tested):** `c = k·v (k>0) ⇒ F_ratio=1 ⇒ F_int=1 ⇒ F_ord=1`.

**Score of one assignment** `r ↦ m(r)`:
```
reward(r) = Σ_{rung ≤ type(r)}  w_rung · F_rung( extract_{m(r)}(figure),  datavec(r) )
```
with weights `w_ord < w_int < w_ratio` so more captured structure always scores higher.

### Ladder height is set by the DATA, never the figure
The number of rungs equals the **data relation's** own scale type, not the measurement's.
- **Sales → 3 rungs** (ordinal ⊂ interval ⊂ ratio); max `w_ord + w_int + w_ratio`.
- **Month/label order → 1 rung** (ordinal only); max `w_ord`. Scoring month-positions for
  *even spacing* would reward inventing interval structure the labels lack — forbidden.
The figure may *over*-provide structure harmlessly to fidelity; the data can never *grant*
a rung it does not have. Rewarding surplus structure is the job of the penalty book (§8),
not the ladder.

Each rung's failure names a chart pathology: wrong order (bottom); right order/wrong
spacing = log-or-√ scale (miss interval); right spacing/wrong zero = truncated baseline
(miss ratio). Dropping the shared baseline is exactly the demotion from ratio to interval.

### Weight calibration (open problem, flagged)
`F_int` (an r²) and `F_ratio` (an exp-of-variance) are not on a common natural unit, so
`w_ord, w_int, w_ratio` do real work. Preferred principled anchor: **Cleveland–McGill
decodability** (position > length > angle > area …) so weights are empirical, not chosen.
Keep them in `config`; treat calibration as a first-class task, not a magic number.

## 7. Assignment (and the invention mode)

An **assignment** maps each data relation to one measurement, **legal iff
`type(data) ≤ stamp(measurement)`** (read a stamp down, never up).

- **FixedAssignment (bars):** sales → length (or height against a posited baseline);
  order → page proj∥ (x-position). Deterministically yields bars. Use first.
- **BestAssignment (invention):** score the figure under the *best legal* assignment,
  `argmax_α reward(α)`. Lets the figure "choose" its encoding; this is where new diagram
  kinds can emerge. Same data relation via `magnitude` (against a central origin) → a
  radial plot; via `proj⊥` (against a baseline) → a dot/lollipop plot.

Both must be pluggable policies behind one interface. Start Fixed; graduate to Best.

## 8. The penalty book (deferred, but architected in now)

Total score is `S = reward(assignment) − Σ penalties`. Penalties are **first-class,
registered terms with configurable weights defaulting to 0** — sewn in at the deepest
level per Principle I, even while switched off.

- **Spuriousness / overencoding:** structure the figure asserts beyond the data — e.g.
  equal spacing read as interval on ordinal labels; any unassigned measurement that varies
  with visible pattern. This is the counterpart to the ladder: the ladder rewards capture,
  this punishes fabrication. Keep on separate books.
- **Frozen degrees of freedom:** measurements assigned no datum (baseline height, tilt)
  should carry no across-line variance. Penalize `Var(baseline) + circularVar(tilt)`.
  Driving these to zero installs the shared baseline / common orientation *without*
  hard-coding "bar chart," and upgrades length-comparison into position-on-a-common-scale.
- **Economy:** penalize count of posited frames and active measurements (Occam pressure).

## 9. Optimization

- **Tractability:** 48 parameters, not millions. This is toy-scale. The score is
  differentiable (the ordinal surrogate is smooth; the one cusp at zero length is repelled
  by `log`). Gradients are cheap; the diagram *is* the variables (optimize coordinates
  directly, like image-space style transfer — there is **no network to train**).
- **Live, not batched:** one step is microseconds; thousands of steps/second; it animates
  in real time. The giant-tensor regime never appears at this scale.
- **Hybrid search (matches the landscape):** the objective is non-convex.
  - *Gradient descent (Adam)* does the smooth local polishing of lengths and positions,
    where it has strong signal (the ratio and frozen-DOF terms).
  - *Evolution / random restarts* handles global structure and discrete choices — the
    assignment, and getting the ordering right — because the ordinal term is flat inside a
    correct ordering (it vetoes inversions, it does not pull).
- **Invariances → the optimum is a valley, not a point:** the score is invariant to
  overall scale `k` and horizontal translation/spacing, so many equivalent bar charts tie.
  This is correct and must be respected by convergence detection (detect score plateau,
  not parameter fixity).

## 10. What "done" looks like for v1

From many random seeds, under FixedAssignment, the evolver reliably converges to bar
charts (up to the known invariances), the fidelity breakdown reads sensibly (sales carrier
climbs all three rungs, order carrier tops its one rung), and every invariant in
`ARCHITECTURE.md §Verification` holds under adversarial tests. Then: turn on the first
penalty term; then BestAssignment; then weight calibration.
